Renewable Technologies






In order to combat climate change, the depletion of fossil fuels, and environmental pollution, renewable energy, or RE, is essential. By offering sustainable substitutes for traditional fossil fuels, renewable energy sources (RES) like solar, wind, biomass, geothermal, and hydropower considerably lower emissions of carbon dioxide (CO₂) and other greenhouse gases (GHGs).The need for energy security, pollution control, and financial gains are what are driving the global transition to renewable energy technology (RET). RES can encourage sustainable development, meet international environmental commitments, and power remote areas.


The successful implementation of RE depends on how the public views and accepts it. Digital platforms and social media offer insightful information on public opinion, which can influence investments in renewable energy and policy. Sentiment analysis of public discourse can disclose awareness, obstacles, and readiness to make the switch to RE. Even though adopting RE presents both financial and technical obstacles, public opinion is a significant factor in decision-making. A cleaner and more sustainable future depends on the development and promotion of RET as climate action gains international attention.
